About This Project

This site examines how religious faith underpinned the Civil Rights Movement, focusing on key figures like Martin Luther King Jr. and organizations such as the Southern Christian Leadership Conference. Through theological rhetoric, sermon excerpts, and gospel music, faith provided both moral guidance and community structure for activism.
